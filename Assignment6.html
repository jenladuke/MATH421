  1. For two continuous variables
  • Scatter Plot
df1 %>% 
  filter(age<50) %>% 
  group_by(age) %>% 
  summarise(ave_loss = mean(as.numeric(capital.loss))) %>% 
  ggplot()+geom_point(aes(x=age, y=ave_loss))

This shows that as the age increases, the average losses also increases.

  • Line Plot
df1 %>% 
  select(age, capital.loss) %>% 
  filter(age<50) %>% 
  group_by(age) %>% 
  summarise(ave_loss = mean(as.numeric(capital.loss))) %>% 
  ggplot()+
  geom_point(aes(x=age, y=ave_loss))+
  geom_line(aes(x=age, y=ave_loss))+
  geom_smooth(aes(x=age, y=ave_loss))

The line plot shows an increase of average loss with many peaks and valleys. The smooth plot depicts the trend without any outliers and and shows the inreasing trend.
